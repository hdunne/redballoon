Topic Objective: Understanding differences between bees.

Honey Bees

  • Only Honey Bees and Bumblebees are eusocial.
  • Other types of bees are solitary.
  • Bumblebees do make honey but very little and of lower quality.

Captions

Why are honey bees the only bees kept for making honey? This is because they are the most efficient at it. Honey bees and bumblebees are the only bees that are eusocial, meaning that they live together in a colony. There are about 50 species of honeybees and bumble bees. The other 950 species of bees are solitary.

Although bumblebees do live in colonies and produce honey, the entire colony save for a lone mated queen dies off in the winter. Because of this, there is no need for bumblebees to create the excess honey to survive the winter like there is in honey bees. In general a bumblebee colony will produce little more than a thimble full of honey at a time. Additionally, as bumblebees are not making honey with the intention of storing it for long periods, it has a higher water consistency and can go bad.
