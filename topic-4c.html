The Hive

  • Langstroth - popular style of hive in the US
  • Bottom Board - hive if placed on top of this
  • Deep Super AKA Brood Chamber - queen, drones, and babies live here
  • Queen excluder - screen that only workers are small enough to pass through
  • Honey Supers AKA Shallow Supers - where honey is stored
  • Inner Cover - bee escape
  • Outer Cover - keeps rain out

Captions

The most common type of hive used in the United States is the Langstroth. Starting from the bottom you have the stand which lifts the hive up off the ground, then you have the bottom board upon which the hive rests.

Your main compartment is known as the Deep Super or the Brood Chamber. This consists of several frames in which the bees will construct their honeycombs. In here will live your queen, the drones, and the brood (immature bees.) It is possible to start with only the Deep Super and add Honey Supers after your hive begins to grow. Once you add Honey Supers you may consider adding a Queen Excluder so that the queen is unable to reach the honey supers and make more brood, this will help control the size of the hive and to a degree prevent swarming as well as make honey extraction easier for you when its time to harvest. Honey Supers will have additional frames for the hive to build in and store honey.

Finally you have your inner cover which has a bee escape that will be used when you want to empty the hive for harvesting and then your outer cover which protects the hive from rain.
