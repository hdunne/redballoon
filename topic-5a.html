Topic Objective:To comprehend honey harvesting procedures.

To harvest the honey, the following equipment is needed:

  • uncapping knife
  • uncapping fork
  • tub
  • honey extractor
  • double sieve
  • food grade bucket
  • containers
Products   Harvesting   Pollination Services   Key Terms

Captions

The best time to harvest the honey is when all the frames are 80% capped and full, and when the nectar is cured. If the nectar is not cured, it will be runny this will result in honey that will spoil or ferment. You don’t want to harvest your honey too late, because the bees will start to eat it. Also, if you wait until it gets cold, the honey will crystalize and it will be difficult to extract.

There are several tools needed to extract the honey:

  • Uncapping knife
    -The uncapping knife needs to be heated, and then slice quickly through the capping.
    -The honey can only be extracted when these seals have been removed.
  • Uncapping fork
    -This tool is used to open sealed cells that haven’t been opened by the uncapping knife.
  • Tub
    -The tub is used for storing the wax.
  • Honey extractor
    -This machinery uses a centrifugal force that spins the combs and the honey flies off. -These extractors come in all shapes, sizes, and costs. -Renting an extractor is also an option.
  • Double sieve
    -This is used to remove the impurities of the honey when the honey is being poured out from the honey extractor.
  • Food grade bucket
    -Use this bucket to place the extracted honey.
  • Containers
    -This is to store honey for keeping or selling.
