Topic Objective: To understand the life cycle of a bee.

Bee Life

  • Egg
    • laid singly in individual cells
    • hatch after 3 days
  • Larvae
    • fed by workers
    • grub-like
    • approx 3 more days depending on role
  • Prepupa
    • workers cap cell with beeswax
    • turn into pupae within hours
  • Pupae
    • doesn't eat
    • capped in cell
    • little movement
    • growing wings
    • chews way out to emerge as an adult
    • entire process from egg to adult 16-24 days depending on role

Captions

Eggs are laid by the queen in individual honeycomb cells. After 3 days they will hatch and are then fed by young worker bees. For the first 3 days they will all be fed royal jelly. After this their diet will change depending on their role. Future queens will have been either laid in or moved to special queen cells and flooded with royal jelly for six days in order to fully develop their ovaries. Future workers will be fed nectar, diluted honey, and pollen. Drones, which come from unfertilized eggs, will be laid in larger cells than females.

The larva will spin cocoons and the worker bees will cap off their cells with beeswax. During this time the pupae will barely move and will not eat at all. They will develop their wings and once they finish growing they will eat their way out, emerging as adult bees. A queen takes about 16 days from egg to adulthood. A worker will take 21 days and a drone around 24 days.
