Captions

Bees are the only insect on earth that produces food in the form of honey. They also produce beeswax, propolis, royal jelly, and bee venom. Honey is not just for sweetening food, but contributes in maintaining the health of the human body. Some of the benefits of honey includes prevention of heart disease, is an anti-inflammatory, increases energy, aids in wound healing, promotes absorption of calcium, and reduces arthritic inflammation.

Beeswax is a very unique product produced by bees and demonstrates specific properties:

  • Beeswax is extremely stable and can last thousands of years.
  • It is waterproof, and was used during world war II to waterproof tents, ammunition, and ignition systems on vehicles.
  • Melting point is between 145 to 150 degrees F, and is one of the natural wax substances with the highest melting point. Beeswax candles are of the highest quality as they do not smoke, or have a disagreeable odor.
  • Beeswax is also used for the following items:
    -Cosmetics
    -Crayons
    -Lubricants
    -Furniture polish
    -Creating dentures and dental crowns
Apitherapy is medicinal products produced by bees. The honey is antibacterial, includes B vitamins, and is used as a salve.

Propolis is a glue produced by bees from beeswax and tree sap, and its’ use has been documented as early as 350 BC. Propolis possesses many unique properties include use as an anti-bacterial, anti-fungal, anti-inflammatory; and contributes to healthy bones, and lowers blood pressure. Propolis contains antioxidants; helpful in fighting cancer cells. Propolis is commonly used in lip balm. Use propolis with caution as it can worsen asthma.

Royal jelly, produced by the bees’ salivary glands, is used for treating fatigue, decreases cholesterol levels, increases host immunity, reduces headaches related to menopause, reduces osteoporosis, and improves skin integrity. Royal jelly also possesses anti-inflammatory properties and is an anti-arthritic, inhibits cancer cell growth, and useful for blood coagulation disorders and aids in regulating hormones.

Bee venom is used in treating cancer, tumors, and HIV. Bee venom is also used in treating blood coagulation disorders, arthritis, and diabetes. Bee venom also demonstrates antiviral, antifungal, and antibacterial properties and is used for treating many health ailments of the human body.
