Topic Objective: Understand the role of bees in pollination.

Saving the Planet

  • Bees pollinate flowers that results in beautifying the planet and creating homes for living organisms.
  • 1/3 of all fruits and vegetables produced are pollinated by honeybees
  • An Estimated 50% to 80% of the world's food supply is directly or indirectly affected by honeybees
  • Cotton, a major resource for the clothing and furnished industries, is pollinated by bees
  • Bee pollination is responsible for more than $15 billion in increased crop value each year

Captions

Honeybees are important to human life. They pollinate crops and produce several items: honey, propolis, royal jelly, and wax. Approximately 80% of the earth’s food chain is tied to the honeybees’ pollination efforts. Health products and medicines are made from what honeybees’ produce. Cotton, pollinated by bees, is used for clothing, home furnishings, etc. Cattle and livestock depend on alfalfa and oats, which are pollinated by honeybees. Honeybees contribute positively to the economic health of a society. Pollination reduces food- scarcity and increases GDP (gross domestic product).
