Roles

  • Queen (reproductive female)
    • controls colony with pheromones
    • lays up to 2,000 eggs per day
    • lives up to 4 years
  • Worker (sterile females)
    • make up the majority of the hive
    • pheromones from the queen supress their ovaries
    • job is determined by their age
    • they live approx. 40 days
    • day 1 to 2: cell cleaning
    • day 3 to 11: nurse worker larvae
    • day 6 to 11: feed royal jelly to queen larvae
    • day 12 to 17: wax production (build cells, store nectar and pollen)
    • day 22 to 42: Forager
  • Drone (only males)
    • form from unfertilized eggs
    • cannot sting

There are three types of bees found in a hive: queen bee, worker bee, and drone bee.

The queen bee has a life span of 3 â€“ 5 years, and she lays eggs; about 2,000 daily. She will only mate one time with the drones, which are male; and she will remain fertile throughout her life span.

Worker bees are all female, but cannot lay eggs. The life span of a worker bee born in the winter is 4-9 months; and if born in the summer; she will only live 6 weeks. A large percentage of bees are worker bees. They have several roles; hive cleaning, nurse, hive repairer, feed the larva, hive guarder, and collector of pollen and nectar. The worker has a barbed stinger, and she can only sting once, which results in death.

The drone is a male, and their only job is to mate with the queen, which happens in summer. They have a life span of 40-50 days. The drone can only mate once, and then they die; as their barbed sex organ gets torn off during mating with the queen. Drones do not have stingers. Since the drones do not mate during the winter, they are driven out of the hive in the fall.
